I completed my Master's and Ph.D. in Computer Science from Carleton University (Ottawa, Canada) under the supervision of B. John Oommen[cite: 5]. I joined the School of Computer Science at the University of Windsor in 2002 as an Assistant Professor, and was promoted to Full Professor in 2013[cite: 5]. My research focuses on developing shallow and deep machine learning frameworks, graph representation learning, and dimensionality reduction models at the fundamental level, alongside applications in single-cell transcriptomics, structural interactomics, biomedical imaging, and recommender systems[cite: 5, 11, 13].

I am a Senior Member of the IEEE[cite: 5], and a member of both the International Association for Pattern Recognition (IAPR) and the International Society for Computational Biology (ISCB)[cite: 5]. I serve as an Associate Editor for the *IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics*[cite: 5] and *Network Modeling Analysis in Health Informatics and Bioinformatics*[cite: 5]. I am also a principal investigator of the Machine Learning in Bioinformatics Lab at the University of Windsor[cite: 5].
