Biography

I completed my Master's and Ph.D. in Computer Science from Carleton University (Ottawa, Canada) under the supervision of B. John Oommen[cite: 5]. I joined the School of Computer Science at the University of Windsor in 2002 as an Assistant Professor, and was promoted to Full Professor in 2013[cite: 5].

My core research interests focus on designing foundational shallow and deep machine learning models, graph representation learning, and dimensionality reduction tools[cite: 5, 11]. We translate these frameworks to analyze high-throughput transcriptomics, single-cell RNA-seq datasets, molecular interactions, and medical image segmentation arrays[cite: 5, 6, 11, 13].

I am a Senior Member of the IEEE[cite: 5], and an Associate Editor for the journal *IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics*[cite: 5]. I also direct the Machine Learning in Bioinformatics Laboratory at the University of Windsor[cite: 5].
