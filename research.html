Core Research Domains

Our laboratory maps computational models to complex molecular networks and engineering problems, focusing primarily on foundational machine learning paradigms and data integration pipelines across seven distinct fields[cite: 13].

1. Single-Cell RNA-Seq, Genomics and Multi-Omics

Investigating mathematical frameworks to partition high-dimensional cellular expressions[cite: 13]. This covers advanced gene selection approaches, cell-type annotation model selections, and parallel clustering pipelines optimized specifically to process heavy transcriptomics and multi-omic data matrices safely and efficiently[cite: 13].

2. Cell-Cell Communication and Molecular Interactions

Developing structural representations and network embeddings to decode ligand-receptor dynamics[cite: 6, 13]. By analyzing physicochemical environments and subgraph embeddings, our methods reconstruct potential protein isoforms and accurately map out molecular interactions between individual cell clusters[cite: 6, 13].

3. Biomedical Imaging and Image Analysis

Designing lightweight architectures for object recognition, segmentation, and feature classification within medical imaging matrices[cite: 13]. Key directions utilize patchwise iterative graph clustering, Delaunay triangulations, and superpixel graph propagation to refine target detection on Computed Tomography (CT) scans and chest radiographs[cite: 13].

4. Recommender Systems

Exploring personalization infrastructures that integrate Graph Attention Networks (GATs) with contemporary Large Language Model (LLM) agents[cite: 13]. This research domain focuses on contextual collaborative filtering, online user feedback simulation environments, and adversarial fraud mitigations such as neural spam review detectors[cite: 13].

5. Hypergraph Neural Networks

Advancing structural deep learning methods beyond pairwise restrictions[cite: 13]. Our research maps multi-node hyperedge topologies to discover functional spatial domains in complex single-cell transcriptomics data while creating polynomial-time construction algorithms under constrained densest subgraphs[cite: 13].

6. Associative Memory Models

Analyzing modern Hopfield networks and spiced covariance frameworks to augment out-of-distribution (OOD) data handling[cite: 13]. These structural models optimize prototype discovery within hidden latent variables while establishing mathematically sound corruption-resistance properties across deep data spaces[cite: 13].

7. Cancer Research and Clinical Disease Diagnostics

Applying machine learning algorithms to isolate next-generation NGS biomarkers and discover driving molecular mutations in highly complex oncological environments[cite: 7, 13]. Projects focus on targeted gene sequencing integration, fecal-microbial metabolomic diagnostic frameworks, and network-based computational drug repurposing across malignant breast, prostate, and colorectal cancer subtypes[cite: 7, 13].
