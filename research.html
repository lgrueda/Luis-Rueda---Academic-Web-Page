Active Research Fields

Our laboratory designs machine learning solutions for multi-scale biological frameworks and computational intelligence systems[cite: 5, 11, 13]. Here is an overview of our seven active categories based on our production pipeline[cite: 13]:

1. Single-Cell RNA-Seq, Genomics and Multi-Omics

Investigating mathematical techniques to partition high-dimensional transcriptomics profiles[cite: 11, 13]. This encompasses pattern-aware gene selection mechanisms, cell-type annotation metrics, and parallel data-clustering implementations[cite: 13].

2. Cell-Cell Communication and Molecular Interactions

Developing structural representations to unpack complex ligand-receptor interactions[cite: 13]. Our methodologies build on molecular contrastive learning and subgraph embedding techniques to predict cellular interfaces accurately[cite: 13].

3. Biomedical Imaging and Image Analysis

Creating lighter machine learning variants for nodule detection and target isolation on medical scans[cite: 13]. This research implements Delaunay triangulation networks, point-cloud optimizations, and positional convolutions to improve diagnostics on chest radiographs and CT scans[cite: 13].

4. Recommender Systems

Constructing contextual collaborative filtering systems and authentication methods[cite: 13]. Our current projects integrate Graph Attention Networks with modern Large Language Models to simulate real-world user feedback spaces and detect review frauds[cite: 13].

5. Hypergraph Neural Networks

Developing non-linear deep models that expand past traditional edge constraints[cite: 13]. This covers multi-node hypergraph systems that resolve cell-type heterophily and spatial domains across single-cell datasets[cite: 13].

6. Associative Memory Models

Analyzing modern Hopfield layouts and latent prototype discovery frameworks[cite: 13]. These models upgrade out-of-distribution tracking and error resistance traits within heavy neural setups[cite: 13].

7. Cancer Research and Clinical Disease Diagnostics

Translating statistical learning frameworks into targeted clinical discovery panels[cite: 7, 13]. Research lines include identifying cross-platform transcriptomics biomarkers for prostate cancer, metabolomic screens for young-onset colon cancer, and sub-type drug repurposing models[cite: 7, 13].
