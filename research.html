Research Fields

Our laboratory designs machine learning solutions for multi-scale biological frameworks and computational intelligence systems[cite: 5, 11, 13]. Our research activities are divided into seven core streams[cite: 13]:

1. Single-Cell RNA-Seq, Genomics and Multi-Omics

Developing robust mathematical models to identify cell-type annotations and handle transcriptomics distributions. Projects focus on pattern-aware gene selection models, manifold learning algorithms, and privacy-preserving framework clusters.

View related publications →

2. Cell-Cell Communication and Molecular Interactions

Formulating subgraph embedding methods to evaluate cell-to-cell communication networks. We investigate contrastive learning platforms to predict protein-protein interaction interfaces and map sequence constraints.

View related publications →

3. Biomedical Imaging and Image Analysis

Designing lightweight graph machine learning models for nodule segmentation and tracking on computed tomography (CT) images. We implement positional convolutions, Delaunay triangulations, and superpixel partitions to decrease false positives.

View related publications →

4. Recommender Systems

Integrating Graph Attention Networks with modern Large Language Model (LLM) agents to enhance end-to-end personalization. Additionally, we explore adversarial systems like neural network detectors for review spam filtering.

View related publications →

5. Hypergraph Neural Networks

Exploring structural hypergraph neural network systems to track spatial domains from single-cell transcriptomics data where relationships extend past basic pairwise connections.

View related publications →

6. Associative Memory Models

Utilizing modern Hopfield neural network architectures to advance out-of-distribution (OOD) processing security, data pattern recovery, and latent prototype discovery.

View related publications →

7. Cancer Research and Clinical Disease Diagnostics

Developing multi-omic data integration pipelines to identify clinical biomarkers and mutations across colorectal, bladder, and breast cancer subtypes.
